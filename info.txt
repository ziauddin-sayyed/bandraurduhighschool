Welcome to Bandra Urdu High School & Junior College, Bandra West — a community-centred school established in 1961 that delivers academic excellence and cultural education across secondary and junior college levels. We combine a strong foundation in Urdu language and literature with the Maharashtra State Board curriculum to prepare students for higher studies and meaningful careers.

About Us

Founded in 1961, Bandra Urdu High School & Junior College began as an Urdu-medium neighbourhood school and has expanded to offer Science, Commerce and vocational (MCVC) streams at the junior college level. Our mission is to provide inclusive and affordable education that fosters multilingual skills (Urdu, English, Marathi), critical thinking, and civic responsibility.

Courses

Secondary (Std V–X / Std I–X) — state-board curriculum: Urdu, English, Marathi, Mathematics, Science, Social Science, IT, Physical Education.
Junior College (Std XI–XII) — Science (Physics, Chemistry, Biology, Maths / Computer Science options), Commerce (Accounts, Business Studies, Economics), Vocational/MCVC subjects (offered as per availability).

Admissions (copy for website)

Admissions are open subject to seat availability. Required documents: birth certificate, previous year mark sheet, transfer/TC (if applicable), proof of residence and passport-size photos. Junior College admissions for specific streams may consider prior academic performance.

Facilities

Well-ventilated classrooms, science labs (Physics, Chemistry, Biology), a computer lab (where available), a library with Urdu/English/Marathi books, multipurpose hall for cultural events and a playground for sports.

Faculty

Experienced state-board teachers with regular in-service training. The school leadership includes a principal and administrative staff who coordinate academics, examinations and student welfare programs.

Extracurricular & Clubs

Urdu literary circle (mushaira), debate and elocution clubs (Urdu/English), cricket and football teams, science & robotics clubs, Scouts & Guides / youth organisations, and vocational clubs (computer literacy, home science, bookkeeping).

Achievements & Alumni

Students have historically performed well in state examinations and participated in district-level cultural and sports competitions. Alumni include professionals in education, medicine, engineering and the arts.
